02 — Premise

The realization that every stranger you pass carries a life as vivid as your own. Sonder is that moment, held in glass — worn close, read by few.

THE NOTES

03 — Composition
01 / TOP Black tea & bergamot

The first pass. Cold-brewed black tea sharpened with bergamot — the smell of a city before it wakes.

02 / HEART Wet concrete & iris

A mineral accord built around petrichor on poured concrete, softened by iris root. Structural, not floral.

03 / BASE Vetiver & smoke

What stays after everything else leaves. Haitian vetiver, cade smoke, a trace of warm skin. Twelve hours deep.

THE CAMPAIGN

04 — Editorial

Shot as objects, not portraits. Nobody in these frames is a person; that is the point of the name.

05 — Object

CUT, NOT
MOULDED.

The bottle is an irregular monolith of obsidian glass — faceted like a hand-cut stone, angled off its own axis, no two edges parallel. It refuses the shelf and settles into the palm.

No label, no cap ornament, no typography on the object itself. Every surface decision was subtractive: what remains is weight, an off-balance stance, and a gloss that reads the room's light back at you.
